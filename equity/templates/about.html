About Equity

Equity is an online driver location service intended to help computer users to locate Operating System drivers for hardware devices that are installed in their computer systems. It is intended to largely serve as an automated online service that other operating system software can utilize without the user explicitly having to perform a search. By submitting certain anonymous information about hardware devices for which no driver is currently loaded, Equity can make driver recommendations for a given Operating System, based upon those drivers and systems that it knows about. Although initially designed to target Linux systems, there is no reason why other Operating Systems cannot also use the service.

What's in a name?

In the early days of the Common Law legal system, there was law and there was equity - an alternative system that evolved from people throwing themselves upon the mercy of the King (via the Chancery) to resolve their issues. These were issues of natural justice for which there was often no good alternative solution, and this parallel legal system existed independently of the regular courts for hundreds of years (before largely being merged into modern day court systems). Just like those royal subjects of olden times, modern computing users must occasionally throw themselves upon the mercy of the internet to find drivers for hardware not supported by their Operating System. In this sense, Equity hopes to be a stop-gap alternative solution until the day when third party driver updates are no longer needed.
